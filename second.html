About Swatties for Immigrant Rights

Swatties for Immigrant Rights comprises of the undocumented and DACAmented student community and their allies that pushes to reform Swarthmore as an equitable institution for every student, regardless of immigration status.

Oswaldo Morales '21

Swatties for Immigrant Rights has digitized the current channels of financial, academic, legal, and mental health support available for undocumented/DACAmented students through the college. This allows undocumented/DACAmented students to navigate institutional support without revealing needing to reveal their immigration status to inquire about eligibility.

Veronica Testi '21

For prospective students, we hope that the website gives you a taste of how being undocumented/DACAmented may influence your academic journey as a Swarthmore student. The website addresses current support available as well as how the institution could evolve in ways to address various shortcomings. We want you to have the fullest purview of what your Swarthmore experience may look like as an undocumented/DACAmented student. Questions? Contact us.

Judith Weng '23

There is no static definition on how to best be an ally to the undocumented/DACAmented community. However, we know it involves an amalgam of being present, listening, advocating and understanding. We encourage everyone continuously educate themselves on what they can do to support their undocumented/DACAmented community, especially here at Swarthmore.
